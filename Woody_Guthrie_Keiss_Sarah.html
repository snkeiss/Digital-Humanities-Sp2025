Woody Guthrie

If the fight gets hot, the songs get hotter. If the going gets tough, the songs get tougher. -Woody Guthrie

Background

Woody Guthrie was an American folk singer born in Okemah, Oklahoma in 1912. After marrying and having three children, the Great Depression impacted his ability to support his family. He decided to hitchhike his way to California to search for better opportunities.

Music Career

After moving to California, he got a job at a radio show where he began to start writing and performing songs. His music grew in popularity after moving to New York in 1940. As anti-communist sentiments grew in the late 1940s and early 1950s, Woody and other folk singers were targeted for their stances on unions and free speech. In 1954, he was diagnosed with Huntington's Disease, following which, he ended his music career. Woody Guthrie died in 1967, but he is still known today as one of the most important folk singers of all time, and as someone who spoke up for the disenfranchised.
